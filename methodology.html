What this is

The School Census is an independent compilation of the number of schools per country, collected by the Sunstone Institute Data Team from national statistical offices, ministries of education, and a handful of international aggregators. We publish two collections so far: a first pass collected in 2024 and a second, wider pass collected in 2026.

The 193 UN member states have been considered for the scope of this dataset.

Read the delta with care. The difference between a country's 2024 and 2026 numbers is labelled a reported difference, not growth. The two collections used different methodologies — for example, the 2026 collection excluded vocational institutes that the 2024 collection sometimes included, and some countries changed what they themselves count as a "school". Treat the delta as directional, not precise.

Sources

Every per-country number on the globe links to its source. Source preference, in order:

  1. A national ministry of education's statistical yearbook or official registry.
  2. A national statistical office's education section.
  3. An international aggregator: UNESCO UIS, World Bank EdStats, OECD.
  4. Peer-reviewed or regional-government reports that cite ministry figures.
  5. Derived estimates (only when no primary source exists, clearly flagged).

Reporting year vs collection year

The two collection labels (2024, 2026) refer to when Sunstone compiled the row, not to the year the source was published. The tooltip and drawer always surface the source's own reporting year (e.g. 2022-23, 2024-25) next to the count, because that's the year the number actually refers to.

Known anomalies

Year reversal — Panama — for Panama, the 2026 collection cites an older reporting year than the 2024 collection did. This is not a data-quality regression: the 2026 pass replaced the previous secondary source (a news article citing MEDUCA) with the ministry's own 2022 official publication, which is a better and more authoritative source even though its reporting year is earlier.
  • 🇵🇦 Panama — 2024 → 2022 (ministry-direct source, intentional)

Panama shows a small "year anomaly" chip in its country drawer (never on the globe itself, to keep the map clean) so readers know the reporting-year dip is expected rather than an oversight.

Corrections

This dataset is open for independent verification. If you spot an error — a missing source, a number that doesn't match the ministry it cites, a mislabelled territory — submit a correction via our Form with the country code, the specific figure, and a link to the source you believe is correct. We aim to respond within five business days.
